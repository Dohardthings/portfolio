Daniel McBryde

I'm a web developer/entrepreneur that got my start as a nuclear plant operator in the US Navy, which led to the offshore oil industry before landing me in the development world. My road to development has been a long and winding one, but I think it gives me a unique perspective that comes in handy. I am a firm believer in the idea that everybody has the responsibilty to live a life worth talking about. I'm passionate about learning and exploration and discovery. I love to travel, and do so as often as possible. Here are a few of the places currently on top of my must visit list!

New Zealand. It's the top of my list, and for good reason. As a hiking enthusiast, its top tier natural beauty and unspoiled wilderness calls to me. If you've never seen pictures, I highly recommend doing a google search.

Spain. I walked across it in 2010, and I loved it so much I may do it again. My facebook has loads of pictures from my Camino de Santiago!

Yellowstone National Park. I know it's not international, but this is a place apart and a hiker's dream. There also may be 1-3 million dollars in hidden treasure there, so that appeals to my adventurous side as well!

What I do

Some of this is Code related, some of it is not. I like to think there is more to life than my work(though I do enjoy my work), and that is represented here.

I'm an Idea guy

I like making things better, and I like fixing what's wrong. That moment of discovery when an idea that could solve a problem comes to me is one of my favorite feelings. I'm not afraid to try new and different things, and just because I don't like or agree with something initailly, doesn't mean it doesn't deserve consideration.

Teacher

I've had experience teaching both in the military (For nuclear pwer plant operators) and since becoming a civilian (as an assistant Front End Instructor at The Iron Yard). I don't consider myself to understand something unless I can communicate it clearly and simply, and teach it to others. I feel this makes me a better team member because it forces me to think out what I'm doing so I can explain it if I need to.

Developer

Up to this point I've mostly done front-end development, but I've dipped my toe into java as well, and am very interested in learning back end, because I want to more fully understand how things work on the server side. As far as frameworks go I've built mostly in Ember, but have done troubleshooting for View JS projects as well. I knowI have more frameworks to learn, and feel like I'll pick them up pretty quickly.

I fix stuff

Code isn't the only place I problem solve. I fix plumbing, cars, and other stuff too. I like fixing things, and like diagnosing the problem even more. I prefer macro problem solving as my talents are best used there, but all problem solving is something I love. From github problems to your toilet not working, I like being the guy who can make it work again.
